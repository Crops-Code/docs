The [Observatory](/concepts/observatory) turns your whole ClusterCode fleet into a navigable galaxy. This guide walks you through finding your way around it — switching how it's grouped, moving the camera, reading live telemetry, and spotting work that's still waiting for a home.

<Steps>

1. ### Open the Observatory

   From the orchestrator, open the **Overview** nav and choose **Observatory**. Your tenant appears at the center, with your [workers](/concepts/workers) as glowing suns and their [DevBoxes](/concepts/containers) orbiting as planets. The view follows your app theme automatically.

2. ### Switch between the Workers and Projects lenses

   Use the segmented toggle at the top right to change how the galaxy is grouped:

   - **Workers** clusters DevBoxes under the worker that hosts them — best for "what's running where?"
   - **Projects** groups bodies by [project](/concepts/projects) as constellations. A worker serving more than one project repeats in each, with a **×N badge** so you know it's the same machine.

   <Aside type="tip">
     Flip to the **Projects** lens to see at a glance how a single project's
     work is spread across your fleet, then back to **Workers** to reason about
     capacity and placement.
   </Aside>

3. ### Pan and zoom to find a resource

   **Drag** anywhere to pan the camera, and **scroll** to zoom — up to **5.5×**. **Hover** any body to read a quick tooltip. The animation pauses near your cursor, so a busy cluster holds still long enough to read.

4. ### Read live stats and jump to the full page

   **Click** a body to open its **detail card**. The card lazily fetches live telemetry the moment it opens:

   - A **worker** shows live **CPU% and cores** plus **used / total memory**.
   - A **running DevBox** shows live **CPU% and memory usage**.

   Every card carries a **deep link** into the full page for that resource — worker, DevBox, [run](/concepts/runs), [schedule](/concepts/schedules), or project — so the Observatory is a fast way to *find* something and jump straight to it.

5. ### Spot unassigned work as comets

   Look for **comets** streaking through the **holding belt** around the center. Each comet is a run or schedule that has **no worker or DevBox yet**. If work isn't getting picked up, this is the first place to check.

   <Aside type="note">
     Remember that only **active** resources orbit — offline workers and stopped
     DevBoxes sit still. A moving galaxy is a busy fleet; a quiet one is idle.
   </Aside>

</Steps>
