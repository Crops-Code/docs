A **Cloud Worker** is a [DevBox](/concepts/runtime-catalog) that ClusterCode hosts
and meters by the hour, so you can run a fleet of agents without owning a single
machine. It's the same DevBox you'd run on your own hardware with
[BYOM](/concepts/workers) — same image, same live terminal, same
[visual desktop](/guides/visual-testing) — just running on infrastructure we
manage.

## Why managed compute

Best-of-N parallel agents, overnight [Schedules](/concepts/schedules), a dozen
DevBoxes at once — they all need machines, and most people don't have a rack of
them sitting idle. Cloud Workers are the on-ramp: spin up Linux DevBoxes from
**$0.06/hour**, or boot a real [Windows VM DevBox](/concepts/windows-containers)
when your stack needs one. Outgrow the cloud and you can point the exact same
DevBox at your own machines with BYOM — nothing in your setup changes.

## Pricing

This is the canonical Cloud Workers price list. All rates are per running hour,
billed against your compute allowance and then your wallet top-up.

| Tier | OS | vCPUs | Memory | Price / hour |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Linux Small | Linux | 1 | 2 GB | **$0.06** |
| Linux Medium | Linux | 2 | 4 GB | **$0.12** |
| Linux Large | Linux | 4 | 8 GB | **$0.24** |
| Windows Small | Windows | 2 | 6 GB | **$0.18** |
| Windows Medium | Windows | 4 | 8 GB | **$0.24** |
| Windows Large | Windows | 6 | 10 GB | **$0.48** |

<Aside type="note">
  Windows tiers run a real Windows VM under the hood (QEMU), so they carry more
  memory overhead and a higher hourly rate than the equivalent Linux tier. See
  [Windows Containers](/concepts/windows-containers) for what that gets you.
</Aside>

## What's included on Starter

Starter is **$9.99/month** and includes **$2 of AI assistance plus $2 of
compute** — no "credits," no mystery units. On the cheapest DevBox, Linux Small
at **$0.06/hour**, that $2 of compute works out to:

```
$2.00 ÷ $0.06/hr  ≈  33 hours
```

So Starter includes **up to ~33 hours** of a running Linux Small DevBox each
month. Larger tiers and Windows DevBoxes cost more per hour, so the same $2 buys
proportionally fewer hours there. Need more of either AI or compute? Top up a
wallet and pay the metered rate — you always see the meter.

<Aside type="tip">
  ~33 hrs is `$2 ÷ $0.06/hr` on a Linux Small DevBox. AI assistance is metered
  **separately** from compute. The per-plan compute allowance derives from each
  plan's included-compute dollars divided by the tier rate — see
  [Plans &amp; Limits](/reference/plans-and-limits) and the
  [Cloud worker guide](/guides/cloud-worker).
</Aside>

## How billing works

- **Metered by the hour** — you pay only for the time a DevBox is actually
  running. Stop it and the meter stops.
- **Allowance first, then wallet** — each plan includes a monthly compute
  allowance; once it's spent, usage draws from your wallet top-up at the metered
  rate.
- **Same DevBox, your machines or ours** — move any workload between Cloud
  Workers and your own [BYOM machines](/concepts/workers) without changing the
  image or the run.

## Measured launch time

Launching a DevBox from a locally-built [golden
image](/guides/build-image-from-container) is fast — the pre-built disk skips the
setup work — but we measure that launch time live rather than quote a number we
haven't timed.

<Aside type="note" title="Launch time is measured, not asserted">
  We don't publish a launch-from-image speed here. The number is measured live in
  the demo and filled in once we've timed it in front of you, rather than
  asserted up front.
</Aside>
